# Self Justification

MathCoding is justified if its own structure demonstrates the difference between:

- prompt-centered development
- artifact-centered development

The repository therefore serves two roles simultaneously:

1. methodology definition
2. worked example of the methodology

This is its fractal property.

The third test: the methodology admits unverifiability honestly. Prose quality, UX tone, strategic prioritization, and ethics cannot be mechanically checked. Rather than excluding them from scope or pretending otherwise, MathCoding names them, declares a reviewer, and turns unverifiability into a documented obligation. The same honesty applies to the methodology's own self-description: `methodology/self-spec/` has a `VERIFIED` model because that model admits mechanical checking, and leaves judgment calls to the named agent.

The fourth test, added later: the methodology names its own seams. Four blind spots — the agent between tool exit and `verification.json` write, the unformalized human review, assumption drift, and the under-validated refinement contract — live in [`Known Limitations`](../00-Meta/Known%20Limitations.md) with stable ids (L1–L4). A methodology that hides what it cannot guarantee is not a methodology; it is a sales pitch.
